Question Set 1: Orbital Mechanics Basics?

Answer: This section covers the basics of orbital velocity, trajectory, and Earth's gravitational pull on the ISS.

What is the primary function of the ISS?

Answer: The ISS serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory, focusing on biology and physics.

How long is the ISS, and how fast does it travel?

Answer: It's about the size of a football field (109m) and travels at 28,000 km/h, orbiting every 90 minutes.

Important mission data was recorded in the last 500 years?

Answer: Focus on Apollo, Mercury, Gemini, and recent Mars missions (Curiosity, Perseverance).

Question Set 2: Deep Space Objects?

Answer: Understand the difference between nebulae, galaxies, black holes, and dark matter/energy concepts.

Answer: 100 km (62 miles) above sea level, defining the boundary between Earth's atmosphere and outer space.

How can fluid physics be effectively tested in space?

Answer: In microgravity, surface tension and capillary action dominate, allowing for unique experiments on fluid dynamics.

What results did the Curiosity rover prove on Mars?

Answer: Confirmed that Mars once had conditions capable of supporting microbial life, with evidence of past water.

Question Set 3: Space Telescope History?

Answer: Review the roles of Hubble, Kepler, James Webb (JWST), and Spitzer in astronomical observation.

What kind of materials are used in spacesuit gloves?

Answer: Multiple layers of synthetic materials (Kevlar, Dacron, Neoprene) for thermal protection and micrometeoroid resistance.

Why is the Cupola important to the ISS?

Answer: Provides a 360-degree view for observing Earth, monitoring external systems, and docking operations.

What is NBL, and how does it simulate space?

Answer: The Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory is a large indoor pool used to train astronauts for spacewalks (EVAs) using neutral buoyancy to simulate weightlessness.

Question Set 4: Human Physiology in Space?

Answer: Learn about bone density loss, muscle atrophy, space radiation effects, and countermeasures (exercise, diet).

What are the key dangers of Long Duration Spaceflight?

Answer: Includes galactic cosmic rays, solar energetic particles, psychological stress, and long-term biological changes.

How does the ISS generate and recycle water?

Answer: Uses the Water Recovery System (WRS) to recycle humidity condensate, urine, and wastewater back into potable water.

What is an adaptation specifically for the Loxley system?

Answer: Focus on the Loxley system's role in advanced life support or atmospheric control within the ISS modules.
